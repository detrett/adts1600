CareWear device

  • — Pulse Monitor
  • — Fall Detector
  • — Emergency Alerts
  • — Medication Reminders
Some electronic watches allow the user to track their heartrate by incorporating a pulse sensor under the watch face or in the leash. We believe this function can be taken further by implementing an algorithm that can detect pulse anomalies (arrhythmia) and send a notification to a designated emergency contact. This eases the need for manual contact in an emergency situation.
Our seniors are the demographic that most suffer from accidental falls. It can have catastrophic consequences and leave them incapacitated, sometimes unable to reach out for help. CareWear includes a fall detecting function to help. By using an accelerometer it can sense the changes of speed often pertaining to those of a fall. When it does, it sends a notification to the emergency contact with the location of the fall included.
The emergency alerts that our watch provides can also be triggered manually. By making use of the emergency button on the top right corner, the wearer can send a text notification to the designated emergency contact.
The final function implemented in CareWear caters to those who suffer from memory issues. Forgetting to take an important medication, as well as overdosing, are all-too-common causes of severe damage or death. CareWear will remind the user to take their medication and allow for the reminder to be dismissed once they have done so. It will keep track of it on its display to make it easier for the wearer to remember whether they have taken it already or not. Should the wearer forget or ignore the notification, an emergency notice is sent to the designated contact.
